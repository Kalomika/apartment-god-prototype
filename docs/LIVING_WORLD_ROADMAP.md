# Apartment God Living World Roadmap

This document preserves the next-layer design direction without overwriting the current playable prototype.

## Core Identity

Apartment God should not become a direct Sims clone. The stronger identity is a guardian-angle living dollhouse where the player nudges people, watches them live, and reads their needs through simple expressive face symbols, body habits, bills, errands, social rituals, and consequences.

The player is not just a puppet master. The player is a guardian, landlord, invisible helper, conscience, household manager, and sometimes temptation. Characters should act on their own when ignored, but the player can intervene through taps, requests, purchases, and environmental changes.

## Visual Language

Use original face glyphs instead of platform emoji art. The current emoji prototype proves the read works, but production art should use our own symbol language.

Character face system:

- Neutral face
- Small smile
- Wide smile
- Calm face
- Annoyed face
- Sad face
- Tired face
- Hungry face
- Affection face
- Playful face
- Phone face
- Full / overfed face
- Sick face
- Warning face

Face symbols should have race and skin-tone customization, but faces should remain stylized enough that the game owns its look. Race selection should affect skin tone, hair shape, clothing palette, and maybe cultural food/clothing/shop options later.

## Habit and Health Systems

The character should change over time based on habits.

Food and body system:

- Eating restores hunger
- Eating too often creates a temporary full reaction
- Overeating repeatedly increases body size
- Higher body size reduces stamina recovery
- Higher body size slows run speed
- Very high body size prevents running
- Exercise and better meals gradually reduce body size

Health consequence system:

- Low hunger, low energy, low hygiene, stress, and poor habits raise health risk
- Serious collapse should not be instant death in the prototype
- Ambulance arrives, stretcher crew removes the character, and the player is locked out of controlling that character for recovery time
- Recovery speed depends on money, insurance tier, and care quality

This should be firm but not cruel. The game teaches balance without becoming a medical simulator.

## Budget, Shopping, and House Upgrades

The laptop or desk computer should become the first shop interface.

Shop categories:

- Furniture
- Floors and carpet
- Wallpaper and paint
- Kitchen appliances
- Bathroom upgrades
- Electronics
- Clothing
- Pet items
- Health items
- Lighting
- Energy-saving upgrades

Purchasing rules:

- Items cost money
- Some items reduce bills
- Some items improve needs faster
- Some items change character behavior
- Upgrades should visually appear in the apartment

Early examples:

- Better fridge, food lasts longer or improves hunger more
- Exercise mat, improves fitness
- Bigger TV, improves fun faster
- Efficient lights, lower electric bill
- Better bed, improves energy faster
- Dog toy, improves dog fun

## Economy and Jobs

The job system should eventually let the player choose a career. Each job should create different schedules, income, stress, social effects, and lifestyle risks.

Starter jobs:

- Delivery worker, flexible but tiring
- Office worker, steady but boring
- Artist, unstable but high fun
- Streamer, fun but inconsistent income
- Nurse, high stress but meaningful
- Tech worker, high pay but sedentary

The goal is not just to earn money. The goal is to create a life rhythm that players shape and then watch play out.

## AI and Imagination Layer

The game should eventually have an AI-assisted shop or request system, but not every second of gameplay should depend on expensive language-model calls.

Good use of AI:

- Player types, “I want a cozy red rug for the bedroom”
- AI maps that to an available item or generates a new item variant
- AI names household items
- AI generates friend dialogue summaries
- AI suggests routines
- AI creates personalized furniture descriptions

Avoid using AI for every character decision. Daily behavior should run on local rules so the game remains fast and affordable.

## Relationship and Social Systems

The girlfriend, dog, friends, future kids, and visitors should have autonomy. The player can request, invite, respond, and influence, but cannot fully puppeteer everyone.

Relationship actions:

- Talk
- Kiss
- Cuddle
- Tickle
- Hold hands
- Watch together
- Cook together
- Ask favor
- Give gift
- Apologize
- Encourage
- Check in

Family-friendly private-time system:

- Characters go to bedroom
- Lights turn off
- Activity bar runs
- Lights turn back on
- Relationship increases
- Repeated use can eventually trigger pregnancy in the simulation

Pregnancy should progress through in-game days or months. During pregnancy, the girlfriend can request help, food, rest, attention, or errands.

## Pet System

The dog should be a full household member, not decoration.

Dog actions:

- Pet
- Feed
- Train
- Fetch
- Play
- Bath
- Sleep

Dog traits later:

- Calm
- Rambunctious
- Clingy
- Protective
- Mischievous

Training should affect messes, responsiveness, and how chaotic the dog becomes while ignored.

## Phone and Social Network

The phone should be a bottom-screen interface.

Phone features:

- Contacts
- Calls
- Messages
- Invite friend
- Work app
- Shop app
- Bank app
- Bills app
- Health app

Random calls:

- Phone bubble rings near the character
- Double tap or menu action answers
- Ignoring it creates a missed call
- Friends and family can invite the player out later

## City Expansion

The apartment is phase one. The larger fantasy is a GPS-like living town where the camera follows the character at the edge of the screen.

Expansion order:

1. Apartment
2. Building hallway
3. Outside sidewalk
4. Corner store
5. Workplace stub
6. Park
7. Friend apartment
8. Small district
9. Full town

The player should be able to zoom out for a wider guardian view or zoom in for character detail.

## How This Becomes Its Own Thing

The difference from The Sims should be clear:

- Less direct puppeteering
- More guardian intervention
- More autonomous household life
- More emoji-like symbolic readability
- More tap-based mobile control
- More visual shorthand
- More life consequence systems
- More AI-assisted customization
- Less micromanagement
- Less menu overload

Working title direction:

- Apartment God
- Guardian Mode
- Tiny Lives
- Moodhouse
- Dollhouse God
- Pocket Utopia
- Little World
- God Mode, only if legally available after name search

## Near-Term Prototype Slice

The next build should not attempt the full city. The next code slice should focus on:

1. Character creator stub
2. Skin tone and shirt color switching
3. Shop menu from laptop
4. Buy a rug, lamp, better bed, and exercise mat
5. Body size and fitness variable
6. Full reaction after overeating
7. Run speed affected by stamina and body size
8. Simple collapse and recovery event

This is enough to prove the loop: live, earn, buy, improve home, shape habits, see consequences.
