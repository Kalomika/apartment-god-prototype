# Research Note, 2026-07-13, Master Bath Vanity Reference

Status: REFERENCE_NOTE
Branch: phaser-migration

## Sources checked

- Better Homes & Gardens bathroom layout guidance notes that layout planning should account for who uses the bathroom, storage, privacy, efficient use of space, vanity configurations, tub location, and fixture clearances.
- General floor plan symbol guidance describes bathroom sinks as basins set into a counter/rectangular outline and showers/tubs as wall based fixture shapes, not loose middle-room props.
- General bathroom definitions distinguish attached primary/en-suite bathrooms from family/guest bathrooms, supporting Kam's direction that the guest/public bath should live in a future separate upstairs section instead of beside the primary suite.

## Applied design rule for the next patch

- The suite foyer sink should become a wall-mounted double vanity/counter with mirror/storage logic, not a freestanding oval sink in the middle of the room.
- Tub, shower, toilet, and vanity should be placed on walls with clear circulation.
- Bedroom egress should use a clear suite foyer/hall opening, not a blocked corner or fake wall gap.
