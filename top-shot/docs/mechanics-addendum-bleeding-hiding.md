# Mechanics Addendum: Bleeding, Hiding, Investigation

## Bleeding

Bleeding is a separate danger from normal vitality damage. Bullets, sword cuts, knife jabs, and arrow stabs can open a wound. The wound does not always end the match instantly, but it creates blood loss that drains vitality over time. This makes retreat after landing a clean wound a valid tactic.

Bandaging does not fully heal the fighter. It mainly stops the blood loss. Vitality can recover only up to the current vitality stage cap.

## Bandaging

A fighter can bandage while hidden, leaning on a wall, or in a desperate visible situation. Hidden bandaging is safer and faster. Visible bandaging is risky because the opponent can see and interrupt. Bandaging should have an Apartment God-style activity meter so the player can see the action building toward completion.

## Timed hiding

Hiding should never allow a fighter to disappear forever. When a fighter enters shadow or secret cover, a hide timer begins. Hiding gives a tactical window to bandage, recover stamina, or reposition. When the timer expires, the fighter automatically rolls, crawls, or steps back out so the match keeps moving.

Future arena themes can include special hiding entries such as under rocks, vents, sewers, crawlspaces, broken walls, ceiling shadows, or tall grass. These can briefly remove the fighter from the opponent's view in multiplayer, while the owning player still knows their fighter is hiding.

## Investigation

If a fighter loses sight of the opponent for too long, they should not freeze. They should cautiously investigate the last known position with a slower careful walk. This creates tension because the hidden fighter can heal, move, or re-enter from cover.

## Spotting cue

When a fighter is seen, an alert cue can pop up like a small exclamation mark. This is a nod to stealth games without needing to show the AI's full invisible sight cone. The player can infer danger from the alert, posture, and behavior.

## Wall support and strafing

A hurt fighter near a wall can lean or brace against it. This can lower the chance of getting hit, steady stamina, or help the fighter stay upright while bleeding or in the purple pain stage. Later, wall leaning can include true wall cover logic, peeking, firing from cover, and supporting the body with a segmented arm rig.

## Multiplayer visibility goal

In a future multiplayer version, if Fighter A is hidden, Fighter B's player should not see Fighter A on their own screen until the fighter is spotted again. On the owning player's screen, the fighter can remain faint, shadowed, or represented by a hidden marker so they still know their own fighter's state.
